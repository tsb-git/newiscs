IS PRIVACY A MYTH ?

INTRODUCTION

Privacy is defined as an option to have secrecy, the state of being free from public attention. Richard Hughes defined privacy as the right of people to conceal information about themselves that others might use to their disadvantage. Privacy has historical roots in ancient Greek philosophical discussions of which Aristotle’s distinction of ‘polis’ (public sphere political life) and ‘oikos’ (private sphere of domestic life) was the most well-known.

The different domains of privacy includes Physical, Behavioral, Psychological, Normative, Digital privacy, etc.

The domain of privacy partially overlaps with security, including the concepts of appropriate use and protection of information. This brings us to our main question “Is Privacy a Myth?”

DIGITAL AGE AND PRIVACY

We are respiring in a Digital Milieu overshadowed by AI technology, where everything ranging from rudimentary critical infrastructures to meticulous space science is driven by 21st century technology This age has seen the sudden rise of information economy, refinements in machine learning followed by an exponential growth in use of data driven, algorithm driven and AI driven technology .This progress has made data an indispensible component of development for any modern interprise. Privacy is one of the biggest problems in this new Electronic age. A force at the heart of the Internet culture wants to know everything about you and 200 million others, and that info is a very valuable asset tempting people to trade and do commerce with. Consequently, entities of all kinds :Commercial, Governmental, Academic, etc. are motivated to collect and analyze as much data as possible for better understanding of people.

Whether innocuous or not, all these data accumulation practices are becoming more invisible as we are lulled into giving up our digital privacy to engage ourselves as consumers in a connected world.

Each time we browse, shop, share, read, like or follow, make a reservation, pay, go online, we submit trackable identifiers that are collected and sold to agencies, app developers, marketers, and many others who profit from knowledge of our behaviour. Whether we are tracked as customers, constituents or research subjects, more of our consumption preferences, geographic movements, biometric informations, and even our DNA is being recorded and interpreted. A common example is when we search anything or the people we are connected to show up as a part of our recommendations on other sites. The big umbrella of Internet knows a lot more about us than we realize.

SUPREME COURT AND PRIVACY

The Supreme Court of India, in a recent judgement reiterated that the Right to Privacy is a fundamental right of every citizen and even took suo moto cognizance regarding the right to privacy as an intrinsic part of Right to Life and personal liberty under Article 21 and as part of the freedoms guaranteed by Part III of the Constitution. The case was referred to the bench in relation to Aadhar and the legality of using biometrics and private data of the people by the government (to ensure national security is not breached).

But private data is being widely tracked by the government and other agencies (commercial entities, miscreants, hackers, financial fraudsters, etc) for other purposes also. Images, videos and satellite tracking systems are ubiquitous. Facial recognition, fingerprint sensors and biometrics are becoming cheaper and more accurate and are being deployed by commercial agents. But it is more of an obstacle than a help for the national security. One such example is the weak Cyber Security of India which results in the flow of army intelligence data as never seen in other countries. Other incidents include the PANAMA PAPER LEAK[3/4/2016] containing data of 11.5 million leaked documents of thousands of officials; The 2019 FB data leak of 533 million users; Cambridge Analytica data Scandal 2016; The recent 7 may 2021 COLONIAL PIPELINE ransomware cyberattack in USA; The use of Dark Web for increasing terrorist and illegal marketing activities and several other malware and cyber attacks makes us think DO we really enjoy veracious privacy?

From 2004-2009,the internet scams rose from 100,000/year to nearly 300,000/year [Internet Scam Statistics]. From 2010-2012,it continued to rise beyond 350,000/ year, with a monetary loss of over $300 million per year!

PRIVACY CONCERNS

Since Edward Snowden leaked various classified info and disclosed the level and depth of global surveillance during 2013 by various US and other government agencies across nations, the right to privacy has indeed become a serious question. At the moment, the balance of power on Internet is highly skewed towards service providers with massive capital resources; while individuals are left with an onerous choice- use the Internet and surrender some measure of data privacy, or otherwise stay marginalized from the society.

METADATA

Metadata is descriptive information that pinpoints your communications and other activities and can be automatically processed to infer secondary info about an individual’s sexual orientation, political and religious views, race, intelligence and personality painting a profound picture of our lives. Microsoft reports 70% of U.S. recruiters have rejected candidates based on internet information about them. This has created a need to control online privacy settings in addition to controlling our online reputations. Although government claims metadata for its security purpose, but the data can be potentially hacked, leaked or even sold in the black market.

GLOBAL SURVEILLANCE

Mass surveillance of entire population across national borders started in the mid 20th century when the UKUSA agreement was enacted by the UK and USA, later expanded to Canada, Australia, New Zealand and other ‘third-party’ nations to create the present ‘Five Eyes’ alliance resulting in the establishment of a global surveillance network, code named “ECHELON”.

PURPOSES:- The April 2013 summary of leaked documents by Snowden showed that other than to combat terrorism, these programs were employed to assess the foreign policy and economic stability of other countries, and to gather “commercial secrets”. In a December 2013 letter to the Brazilian government, Snowden wrote that “these programs were never about terrorism, they are about economic spying, social control, and diplomatic manipulation. They are about power.

POLITICAL ESPIONAGE

The April 2013 summary disclosed NSA’s “intelligence priorities” on a scale of highest interest to lowest interest which classified about 30 countries as “3rd parties”, with whom it cooperates but also spies on, of which the main targets were China, Russia, Iran, Pakistan, and Afganistan. Other prominent targets were members and adherents of Internet groups and potential whistleblowers. Snowden said the NSA targeted reporters who wrote critically about the US government after 9/11.

As a part of joint operation with the Central Intelligence Agency the NSA deployed secret eavesdropping posts in 8 US Embassies and consultants worldwide. The 2013 data leak also revealed that many intergovernmental organizations, diplomatic missions, and government ministries including Embassy of India in Washington DC and Permanent representative of India to the UN have been subjected to surveillance by the “FIVE EYES”. They collect metadata, infiltrate VPNs, copy entire hard drive disks, hack e-mail, install covert listening devices, and use many other methods to SPY on the agencies and know everything about any country, providing them with tremendous amount of power over us.

SURVEILLANCE EVASION

Several Nations have eluded global surveillance by constructing secret bunker facilities deep below the earth’s surface. Despite North Korea being a priority target, NSA’s documents acknowledged it did not know much about Kim Jong-un’s regime. Libya evaded surveillance by building buried bunkers 40 feet below ground level. Iran’s police chief Esmail Ahmadi in Oct 2012 alleged Google to be a spying tool for Western intelligence agencies; six months later; in April 2013, the country announced plans to introduce an “Islamic GOOGLE Earth” to avoid global surveillance.

HOW TO CONTROL YOUR PRIVACY?

Although controlling our data is technically challenging or either we don’t bother, but if we want to ensure our privacy we can take some steps to protect and avoid our data contravansion to a large extent. These include using a high-quality(paid) VPN service, turning off WebRTC in browser, logons with difficult passwords, antivirus protection, a firewall, using ad blockers, installing Privacy Badger, and using something called NoScript. In addition we can encrypt our e-mails via S/MIME or PGP. Anonymizing proxies or networks like I2P and Tor can be used to prevent Internet service providers from knowing which sites we visit and with whom we communicate. Signal app is notable for being available on many devices and implementing a form of perfect forward secrecy. So, keeping yourself private from others may be challenging but not impossible.

SYNOPSIS

An archaic saying goes by, ‘Democracy and privacy are myth’. If you participate in society to any degreeprivacy is an illusion. Governments will need to go after terrorist organizations to ensure national security. Miscreants, hackers and financial and data scientists will continue to mine high levels of private data. Though most countries give citizens right to privacy in their constitutions and there are international privacy agreements, they are not as plausible as they seem. In effect, securing right to privacy of individuals sounds like a myth. At best, what can be done is to secure individuals’ data by taking privacy measures at one’s own level.
